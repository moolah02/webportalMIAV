1. Getting Started — Login & Navigation

Logging In

1
Open the system URL in your browser. You will be redirected to the login page automatically.
2
Enter your email address and password. These are set by your administrator during onboarding.
3
Click Sign In. If your credentials are correct and your account is active, you will be taken to your dashboard.
Note: If you see "Your account is inactive", contact your administrator. Inactive accounts cannot log in even with the correct password.

The Navigation Sidebar

The left-hand sidebar is your main navigation. It is always visible and divided into sections. Click any section header to expand or collapse it. The section containing your current page is automatically expanded when you load the page.

Sidebar SectionWhat You Can Do There
Company DashboardSystem-wide metrics, alerts, terminal health overview
Employee DashboardYour personal stats, schedule, and quick links
Assets ManagementInternal assets, POS terminals, asset requests & approvals, business licenses
Field OperationsTerminal deployment, job assignments, site visits, support tickets
Project ManagementProjects, project closure and reports
Client ManagementClient directory and per-client dashboards
Employee ManagementEmployee directory and role management
AdministrationSystem settings (departments, categories, service types)
Reports & AnalyticsReports dashboard, technician visits report, Report Builder
Documentation HubOpens the public documentation site in a new tab
My AccountView/edit your profile and sign out
Menu items only appear if you have the required permission. If you cannot see a section, ask your administrator to check your role's permissions.

Top Header Bar

The header at the top of every page shows:

  • Page title — The name of the current section.
  • Your name and role badge — Top-right corner. Click your name to go to your profile.
  • Flash messages — Green (success), red (error), or yellow (warning) banners appear at the top after any action (saving, deleting, etc.). They disappear automatically after a few seconds.

General UI Conventions

  • Blue buttons are primary actions (Save, Create, Submit).
  • Grey/white buttons are secondary or cancel actions.
  • Red/danger buttons are destructive actions (Delete). You will always be asked to confirm.
  • Status badges are colour-coded chips: green = active/completed, orange = warning/pending, red = expired/faulty, grey = inactive.
  • Pagination appears below large lists. Use the numbered buttons to move between pages.
  • Search & filter bars are at the top of every list view. Press the Filter button or hit Enter to apply.

2. Company Dashboard

The Company Dashboard gives management a real-time overview of the entire operation. Access it from the top of the sidebar.

Requires the Company Dashboard (view_dashboard) permission or Super Admin access.

Top Metrics Row

Four large stat cards show at a glance:

  • Total Terminals — All POS terminals in the system, plus how many were added this month.
  • Active Terminals — Currently active terminals and their percentage of total.
  • Offline Terminals — Terminals with status "Offline".
  • Need Attention — Combined count of Offline + Faulty + Maintenance terminals.

Terminal Status Breakdown

Five smaller cards show counts for each terminal status: Active, Offline, Maintenance, Faulty, and Decommissioned.

License Stats Row

Shows: Total Licenses, Active, Expired, Expiring Soon (within 15 days), Critical Priority Licenses, Annual Renewal Cost, and Compliance Rate (%).

System Alerts Panel

Alerts are automatically generated from live data. They are colour-coded by severity:

  • Critical — Expired business licenses, faulty terminals. Require immediate action.
  • Warning — Licenses expiring within 30 days, many offline terminals, contracts expiring soon, low compliance rate, overdue jobs.
  • Info — High-cost renewals approaching, many pending asset requests.

Click any alert to navigate to the relevant section.

Regional Distribution

A table showing every region in the system with: total terminals, active terminals, issues count, and uptime %.

Monthly Trends

A 6-month chart showing terminals added, clients added, and licenses added per month.

Recent Activity Feed

Up to 15 recent events: new terminals, new clients, licenses added or renewed, job assignments, technician visits. Each item shows the time elapsed since the event.

Top Clients

The 5 clients with the most terminals, showing their contract status.

Upcoming License Renewals

The next 10 licenses expiring within 60 days, showing department, responsible employee, and exact expiry date.

5. POS Terminal Management

POS Terminals are the point-of-sale devices that Revival Technologies manages on behalf of clients at merchant locations.

Requires manage_terminals or view_terminals.

5a. Adding & Editing Terminals

1
Go to Assets Management → POS Terminals, then click "+ Add Terminal".
2
Enter the Terminal ID — the merchant's own terminal identifier. Must be unique.
3
Select the Client this terminal belongs to.
4
Enter Merchant details: Merchant Name, Contact Person, Phone, Email.
5
Enter Location: Physical Address, Region, City, Province.
6
Enter Hardware details: Terminal Model, Serial Number, Business Type.
7
Enter Service dates: Installation Date, Last Service Date, Next Service Due.
8
Set Status: Active, Offline, Faulty, or Maintenance.
9
Add Contract Details if applicable, then click Save.

5b. Bulk Import from Excel/CSV

To add many terminals at once, use the import feature.

1
From the POS Terminals list, click "Import".
2
Select the Client these terminals belong to.
3
Upload your Excel or CSV file. The file should have headers in the first row.
4
Map Columns. The system shows your file's column headers. Drag or select each one to match the correct field in the system (e.g., map "TID" → Terminal ID, "Merchant" → Merchant Name).
5
Save the column mapping with a name so you can reuse it next time for the same client format.
6
Click Import. The system will process the file and report how many terminals were created or skipped (duplicates).
If you import from the same client regularly with the same file format, you can load a saved column mapping profile to skip the mapping step entirely.

5c. Terminal Status & Service Tracking

From any terminal's detail page, you can:

  • Change Status — Quickly update the terminal status using the status dropdown.
  • Create Ticket — Raise a support ticket directly from the terminal page.
  • Schedule Service — Set the next service date.
  • Add Note — Add a free-text note to the terminal record.
  • Export — From the list view, click Export to download a CSV of all terminals (respecting any active filters).

Filtering Terminals

The list view supports filters for: Client, Status (Active/Offline/Faulty/Maintenance), Region, City, and Province. Combine multiple filters to drill down to specific subsets.

Service Due Alerts

Terminals are flagged automatically:

  • Due Soon — Service due within 60 days.
  • Overdue — Service overdue by more than 90 days.
  • Never Serviced — Installed but no service date recorded.

6. Internal Assets

Internal Assets track everything Revival Technologies owns: vehicles, IT equipment, software licenses, and more.

Requires manage_assets or view_assets.

6a. Asset Inventory

Go to Assets Management → Internal Assets. The page has four tabs:

  • Assets — The full inventory with search and filters by category, status, and stock level.
  • Assignments — All assets currently assigned to employees.
  • History — Past assignments (returned, transferred, lost, damaged).
  • Assign — Quickly assign an available asset to an employee.

Creating an Asset

1
On the Assets tab, click "+ Add Asset".
2
Select a Category. The form will show extra fields specific to that category (e.g., vehicles show license plate and engine number; IT equipment shows RAM and processor).
3
Fill in the core fields: Name (required), Brand, Model, SKU (unique identifier), Barcode, Unit Price, Currency.
4
Set stock quantities: Stock Quantity (how many you have) and Min Stock Level (triggers a low-stock alert when quantity falls to this number).
5
Set flags: Is Requestable (appears in the self-service catalog for employees to request) and Requires Approval (requests need a manager to approve).
6
Click Save.

6b. Assigning, Returning & Transferring Assets

Assigning an Asset to an Employee

1
Go to the Assign tab or open the asset and click "Assign".
2
Select the Employee to assign to.
3
Set the Assignment Date and optionally an Expected Return Date.
4
Set the Condition at time of assignment: New, Good, Fair, or Poor.
5
Click Assign. The asset's assigned quantity increases automatically.

Returning an Asset

1
Go to the Assignments tab, find the assignment, and click "Return".
2
Set the Return Condition: Available, Maintenance Needed, Damaged, or Retired.

Click Confirm Return. The assignment is closed and the history is preserved.

Transferring an Asset Between Employees

On the Assignments tab, click "Transfer" next to an assignment. Select the new employee. The system closes the current assignment (marking it as "transferred") and creates a new assignment to the target employee in one step.

10. Site Visits

Site Visits are records of completed technician visits to merchant locations. They are primarily submitted via the mobile app and appear in the web portal for review and reporting.

Requires manage_visits or view_visits.

Viewing Site Visits

Go to Field Operations → Site Visits. Use the filters to search by:

  • Merchant name
  • Employee (technician) name
  • Terminal ID
  • Terminal Status (as recorded at the time of the visit)
  • Date range (From / To)
  • Free text search (summary or action points)

Click any visit to open the detail view, which shows the full visit summary, action points, the technician's notes, and a snapshot of the terminal's state at the time of the visit.

Because terminal data is snapshotted at visit time, the visit record preserves history even if the terminal's details change later. This makes visits a reliable audit trail.

14. Roles & Permissions

Roles define what employees can see and do in the system. Each role is a named set of permissions.

Requires manage_roles permission.

Understanding the System

  • Every employee has at least one role.
  • An employee can have multiple roles simultaneously — they get the combined permissions of all their roles.
  • If a role has the All Permissions (Super Admin) permission, it bypasses all other checks.
  • System roles (super_admin, admin, manager, employee, technician) cannot be deleted.

Creating a Role

1
Go to Employee Management → Role Management and click "+ Create New Role".
2
Enter a Role Name. Use lowercase with underscores (e.g., field_supervisor). Must be unique.
3
Check the permissions this role should have. Permissions are grouped by category — expand each category to see individual permissions.
4
Click Save Role.

Cloning a Role

Open any role and click "Clone Role" to create an exact duplicate you can then edit. Useful for creating similar roles with slightly different permissions.

16c. Report Builder — Full Step-by-Step Guide

The Report Builder is a powerful, flexible tool that lets you create any custom report by selecting data sources, choosing columns, applying filters, and exporting results — all without writing any code.

Requires use_report_builder permission. Saving shared templates also requires manage-report-templates.

Go to Reports & Analytics → Report Builder.

Overview of the Interface

The Report Builder page has three main areas:

  • Filter Strip (top) — Choose your data source, apply global filters (region, client, date range), set a row limit.
  • Left Panel — Field Browser — All available columns grouped by table, with a search box. Drag or double-click fields to add them to your report.
  • Right Panel — Report Columns & Results — Shows your selected columns, then the report results table after you click Run.

Step 1 — Select a Data Source

The Data Source dropdown at the top left defines the primary table your report starts from. Available data sources are:

Data SourceWhat It Contains
POS TerminalsAll merchant terminal records (ID, model, status, region, client, service dates)
ClientsAll client company records (name, region, status, contract dates)
RegionsRegion names and codes
VisitsSite visit records (merchant, date, summary, action points, employee)
Visit TerminalsTerminal-level data captured inside each visit
TicketsSupport ticket records (title, priority, status, issue type, terminal, client)
Job AssignmentsAll field job assignments (technician, region, status, service type, dates)
ProjectsProject records (name, client, status, dates)
Changing the data source clears all selected columns. You will see a confirmation dialog before this happens so you can cancel if needed.

Step 2 — Apply Global Filters (Optional)

Before selecting columns, you can narrow down the data using the filter strip:

FilterHow to Use
RegionSelect a specific region to restrict results to that region only.
ClientSelect a specific client to see only their data.
Date ColumnChoose which date field to filter on (e.g., "Job Assignments > Scheduled Date", "Visits > Completed At"). The dropdown shows all date fields from all related tables.
From / ToSet a date range. Only available after selecting a Date Column. Leave blank for no date filter.
RowsMaximum number of rows to return. Default is 100. Set higher for larger exports (up to 10,000). For unlimited rows when exporting, use the Export button (not Run Report).
Leave filters blank to see all data. You can always add filters after seeing initial results.

Step 3 — Add Columns from the Field Browser

The Field Browser panel on the left shows all available columns grouped by table. Each field has a colour-coded chip:

  • Dimension — Text, date, or category fields. These describe what you are looking at (e.g., merchant name, region, status).
  • Measure — Numeric or ID fields that can be counted, summed, or averaged (e.g., terminal ID for counting, revenue amount for summing).

To add a field to your report, use either method:

  • Double-click the field name in the left panel.
  • Drag and drop the field into the "Report Columns" drop zone on the right.

To search for a specific field: Type in the search box at the top of the Field Browser. The list filters in real time as you type.

You can add columns from multiple tables in a single report. The system automatically works out the joins — you do not need to do this yourself. For example, you can add "Merchant Name" (from POS Terminals) and "Company Name" (from Clients) in the same report when starting from POS Terminals.

Step 4 — Configure Columns (Aggregates)

Your selected columns appear as pills in the Report Columns bar at the top right. For each column:

  • Click the × button inside the pill to remove a column.
  • For Measure (numeric) columns, a small dropdown appears inside the pill to set an aggregate function:
AggregateWhat It DoesExample Use
RawShows the raw value for each row (no grouping)Show each terminal's ID
COUNTCounts the number of recordsHow many terminals per region
SUMAdds up numeric valuesTotal revenue per client
AVGCalculates the averageAverage job duration per technician
MINFinds the smallest valueEarliest installation date per region
MAXFinds the largest valueLatest service date per client
Automatic GROUP BY: As soon as you set any column to COUNT, SUM, AVG, MIN, or MAX, the system automatically groups results by all the non-aggregated (Dimension) columns in your report. You do not need to configure this manually.

Example — Terminals per Region

  1. Data Source: POS Terminals
  2. Add column: Region (Dimension)
  3. Add column: Terminal ID (Measure) → set aggregate to COUNT
  4. Click Run Report
  5. Result: a two-column table showing each region and how many terminals it has.

Example — Active vs Offline Terminals per Client

  1. Data Source: POS Terminals
  2. Add: Client → Company Name (Dimension)
  3. Add: POS Terminals → Status (Dimension)
  4. Add: POS Terminals → Terminal ID (Measure) → set to COUNT
  5. Click Run Report
  6. Result: one row per client+status combination, showing the count for each.

Example — Jobs Completed Per Technician This Month

  1. Data Source: Job Assignments
  2. Filters: Date Column = Job Assignments → Scheduled Date, From = first day of current month, To = today.
  3. Add: Job Assignments → Technician ID (Dimension) — this will show the ID. Cross-reference with the Employees list, or add an employee name column if available.
  4. Add: Job Assignments → Status (Dimension)
  5. Add: Job Assignments → Assignment ID (Measure) → set to COUNT
  6. Click Run Report
  7. Result: rows showing each technician × status combination with a count of assignments.

Step 5 — Run the Report

1
Make sure at least one column is selected. If no columns are selected, you will see a validation message.
2
Click the blue "▶ Run Report" button.
3
A loading spinner appears while the system queries the database.
4
The results table appears below the columns bar. The top of the table shows the row count and column count.
5
Scroll horizontally if you have many columns. The column headers are sticky (they stay visible as you scroll down).

Report Builder Tips & Common Questions

Q: Can I combine data from multiple tables in one report?
Yes. Select columns from different tables freely. The system automatically detects and applies the required joins. For example, starting from POS Terminals you can freely add columns from Clients, Regions, Tickets, and Job Assignments.

Q: Why is my column showing IDs instead of names?
Some columns are stored as numeric IDs (e.g., Technician ID, Client ID). Add the corresponding name column from the related table (e.g., add Clients → Company Name to see the client name instead of the ID number).

Q: Why do I get fewer rows than expected when I use COUNT?
When you use aggregate functions, rows are grouped by your Dimension columns. Each unique combination of dimension values becomes one row. This is expected behaviour — you are seeing one row per group, not one row per record.

Q: The report returns zero rows. What went wrong?
Check your filters — a very narrow date range or a region/client filter may be excluding all data. Try removing filters one at a time to identify which one is causing the issue.

Q: Can I sort the results?
Sorting can be done after exporting to CSV/Excel. In-browser sorting is not yet available in the Report Builder.

Q: How many rows can I export?
The screen preview defaults to 100 rows (configurable up to 10,000). The CSV export fetches all matching rows with no limit.

18. Troubleshooting & FAQs

Login Issues

I can't log in with the correct password.
Your account may be inactive. Contact your administrator to check your account status.

I forgot my password.
Contact your administrator. They can set a new temporary password from the Employee Management page.

Missing Menu Items

I can't see a section in the sidebar.
Menu items are hidden if you do not have the required permission. Ask your administrator to review your role and add the necessary permissions.

Page Shows an Error

I see "403 Forbidden" or "Unauthorized".
You are trying to access a page that requires a permission you do not have. Contact your administrator.

I see "500 Server Error".
This is an unexpected system error. Note the page you were on and what you were doing, then report it to your administrator or system support team.

Report Builder Issues

My report returns no results.
Check your filters. A date range that excludes all records is the most common cause. Try removing filters one by one until data appears.

I get a "validation error" when I run a report.
Ensure at least one column is selected. Also ensure your date filter has a valid "From" and "To" date if a date column is selected.

Column values are showing IDs instead of readable names.
Add the human-readable column from the related table. For example, to see client names instead of client IDs, add "Clients → Company Name" from the Field Browser.

Business Licenses

My license create form reloads without saving.
Make sure you are on the correct tab (Company-Held or Customer-Issued) before clicking Add License. The form direction must match.

POS Terminal Import

My import file is rejected.
Ensure the file is .xlsx, .xls, or .csv format. Files must have headers in the first row. Maximum recommended size is 5,000 rows per import.

Terminals are being skipped during import.
Terminals with duplicate Terminal IDs (already in the system) are automatically skipped to prevent duplicates. The import summary shows how many were skipped.
